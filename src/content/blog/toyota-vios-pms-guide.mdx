The **Toyota Vios** is the default first car of the Philippines for one big reason: it's cheap and easy to keep alive. But "cheap to maintain" doesn't mean "maintenance-free" — sundin mo pa rin ang PMS schedule. Here's exactly what a Vios costs to service, interval by interval, plus the wear items to watch. This is the Vios-specific companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Vios runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A regular Vios PMS at casa is about **₱5,000–7,000** (often the lower end); a major 40K–80K service is **₱10,000–15,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- It's **one of the cheapest cars to maintain in PH** — parts everywhere, kahit aling talyer kaya.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.
- Best-in-class **resale** — kumpletong service records protect that value.

## Toyota Vios PMS Cost by Interval

Same logic as any car — costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than just oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Vios) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱5,000–7,000 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–15,000+ |

The Vios usually lands at the **lower end** of the sedan range — its 1.3L engine only needs about 3.5L of oil, so a basic change-oil PMS is light on the wallet. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear Items on a Vios

The Vios is famously bulletproof, pero may konting bagay pa ring babantayan:

- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy to replace.
- **CVT fluid** (CVT units) — many owners change it ~every 40,000 km for long-term smoothness.
- **Engine & transmission mounts** — can soften over the years; a source of vibration.
- **Aircon** — PH heat works the system hard; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.
- **Pang-ilalim** — bushings, tie rod ends, and stabilizer links take a beating on our roads. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` over bumps — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Casa vs. Talyer for a Vios

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (and keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work.

The Vios's superpower here is **parts availability** — genuine and quality aftermarket parts are everywhere, at presyong abot-kaya. Walang shop sa Pilipinas na hindi marunong mag-Vios, kaya wala kang bargaining-power problem.

## What You Can DIY

To save a little between visits: check your tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave brake work, CVT fluid, and anything warranty-related to the pros.

## Verdict: Among the Cheapest Cars to Own in PH

If low running cost is your priority, the Vios is hard to beat — modest PMS bills, a timing chain (no belt drama), parts on every corner, and the best resale value in its class. Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
