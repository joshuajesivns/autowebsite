The **Honda City** is the Vios's classic rival — a bit more refined, powered by Honda's 1.5L i-VTEC, and famous for one money-saving quirk: **a longer PMS interval than most rivals.** It's reliable and reasonable to maintain, with **one rule you can't ignore: the CVT fluid.** Here's the full PMS breakdown. This is the Honda City companion to our [complete PMS schedule and price guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- Honda PH runs a **10,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle — **longer than the 5,000 km interval** at Toyota and Mitsubishi, so fewer casa trips per year.
- A light/standard City PMS is about **₱5,000–7,000**; a major 40K–80K service is more. *(Average casa figures, June 2026 — verify with your dealer.)*
- **Honda parts cost a little more than Toyota's** — but not dramatically.
- **CVT fluid = the non-negotiable.** Use genuine **HCF-2** and service it on schedule.
- 1.5L i-VTEC (L15 family), **119 hp / 145 Nm**, naturally aspirated — the PH **RS is not a turbo.**
- **Timing chain, not belt**, plus a reputation for holding value well.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2020–2026, 5th-gen GN)

This guide covers the **5th-generation City (GN platform)**, launched in the Philippines in **October 2020** and given a cosmetic-only facelift in **July 2023** — same 1.5L i-VTEC engine and CVT throughout, so the schedule and costs here apply across 2020–2026 units alike, including the **30th Anniversary Pearl Edition** special-edition variant launched in February 2026. There's no all-new City generation on sale in PH yet to confuse this guide with.

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 1.5L (1,498cc) i-VTEC, L15 family, 4-cylinder DOHC |
| Power | 119 hp @ 6,600 rpm / 145 Nm @ 4,300 rpm (naturally aspirated) |
| Oil capacity | ~3.5 liters (with filter), 0W-20 |

| CVT fluid | Honda HCF-2 (genuine only) |

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the City

Most City visits are **light PMS** — oil + filter and a general inspection, every 10,000 km. The **heavy (major) PMS** at **40,000 km and 80,000 km** is where the bill grows: brake fluid, coolant, air and fuel filters, spark plugs, and — most importantly — the **CVT fluid (HCF-2)**. The CVT service is the one not to defer; it's cheap insurance against the single most expensive repair on the car.

## Known Issues on the Honda City

- **CVT behavior.** A slight "rubber-band" feel or sluggishness under sudden acceleration is normal for the CVT; persistent hesitation usually means the fluid is overdue. Stick to HCF-2, on schedule.
- **Fuel-pump recall (2018–2021 units).** Honda Cars Philippines recalled select City units for a low-pressure fuel pump that could fail to start or stall — the City had the most affected units. The fix is a free replacement; if you're buying used, confirm it was done.
- **Low ground clearance (134 mm).** The City sits low, so steep humps, ramps, and flooded roads can scrape the pang-ilalim. Dahan-dahan sa mga hump at baha.
- **Interior rattles & highway noise.** Some cabin plastic rattles over time, and wind/road noise picks up above ~110 km/h — typical for the segment.
- **Pang-ilalim.** Bushings, tie rods, and links wear on rough roads. Listen for `lagutok`, `kalog`, and `kagat`; we walk through what those sounds usually mean in our [rundown on diagnosing car noises by ear](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

Under warranty → **Honda casa** (and insist on genuine HCF-2). Out of warranty → a trusted talyer is cheaper for general work, but still use the correct fluids. Deciding between this and the Toyota? Compare with the [Vios PMS guide](/blog/toyota-vios-pms-guide).

## Verdict

The City is a refined, reliable daily that holds its value well, with reasonable upkeep made easier by Honda's longer 10,000 km service interval. It's **still on sale new in 2026** (E, S, V, and RS, all CVT with Honda SENSING standard). The only thing standing between you and a long, happy ownership is **respecting the CVT-fluid schedule** — do that, keep your records, and it'll reward you.
