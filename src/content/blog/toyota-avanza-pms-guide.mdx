The **Toyota Avanza** is the budget 7-seater that lets a whole family move on a small-car maintenance bill. It's gas, not diesel, so servicing it is closer to a Vios than an Innova — mura at simple. The all-new 2022 Avanza was a big change under the skin: it switched from the old truck-style **rear-wheel drive** to a modern **front-wheel-drive** layout with a CVT. Here's exactly what an Avanza costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the one thing to know about which generation you own. This is the Avanza companion to our [full car PMS and cost guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Avanza runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A **light/standard PMS** at casa is about **₱3,600–4,900**; the first PMS is **₱1,500–2,100**. *(Average casa estimates, June 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- The **heavy/major PMS** (40K & 80K km) is the big-bill stop — roughly **₱7,200–8,600 at 40,000 km** and **₱11,400–12,800 at 80,000 km**.
- It's **one of the cheapest 7-seaters to maintain in PH** — gas engine, cheap parts, kahit aling talyer kaya.
- **Know your generation:** up to early 2022 is RWD with a 4-speed AT; 2022+ is FWD with a CVT — different fluids to watch.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Toyota Avanza PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Avanza) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱1,500–2,100 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱3,600–4,900 |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱7,200–12,800 |

The Avanza sits near the small-sedan range — a modest gas engine means a light change-oil PMS. These are auto-media casa estimates (labor + parts, VAT-inclusive); your exact bill varies by variant and dealer, so for a model-specific figure use Toyota's **maintenance cost calculator** at toyota.com.ph. ## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Avanza

Most Avanza visits are **light PMS** — engine oil + filter, tire rotation, and a general check, done every 5,000 km. They're quick and cheap because the 1.3L/1.5L only sips about 3.3L of oil.

The **heavy (major) PMS** lands at **40,000 km and 80,000 km**. On top of the oil change, the casa replaces the items that only come due occasionally — air and fuel filters, brake fluid, the CVT/automatic fluid, and spark plugs. That's why the 40K bill roughly doubles a light PMS, and 80K climbs higher still. Budget for the 40K and 80K stops in advance para hindi ka ma-shock. One generation note: the **new FWD car needs its CVT fluid** watched (change it around 40,000 km for long-term smoothness), while the **old RWD car has rear differential & transmission oil** the FWD models don't — an extra fluid to check periodically.

## Known Issues on the Toyota Avanza

No car is perfect, even a cheap-to-run one. The recurring, owner- and reviewer-reported notes on the Avanza:

- **Underpowered when fully loaded.** Both the 1.3L and 1.5L feel strained with seven aboard plus aircon. On the new car the **CVT can feel hesitant under load and on uphills**, and the old 4-speed AT could feel jerky — reviewers treat this as a characteristic of a small-engined budget MPV, *not* a failure. No widespread CVT-failure reports on the Avanza.
- **Cabin noise & firm ride (NVH).** Thin sound insulation means road and tire noise at highway speed, and the ride can feel firm or bouncy on rough roads — more so on the older RWD car than the newer FWD one.
- **Old RWD body roll & vague steering.** The pre-2022 ladder-frame RWD Avanza felt tall and bouncy with light steering; the 2022+ FWD monocoque is noticeably more composed. Worth knowing if you're buying used.
- **Suspension & bushings under load.** A loaded 7-seater works the pang-ilalim hard on PH roads — listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` over bumps. Full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Check for open recalls.** The pre-2022 RWD Avanza (built Oct 2017–Jul 2019) was part of Toyota PH's **Denso fuel-pump recall**; the 2022+ FWD car had **front shock-absorber** and **side-impact beam** recalls in 2024. All are fixed free at Toyota — confirm your VIN with the dealer.

None of these are deal-breakers — they're the normal trade-offs of an affordable 7-seater, and the recalls are free to resolve.

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work. Like the Vios, the Avanza's superpower is **parts availability** — walang shop sa Pilipinas na hindi marunong mag-Avanza, kaya wala kang sourcing o bargaining problem. Between visits you can DIY the basics: tire pressure, washer fluid, cabin filter, and an oil-level eyeball; leave CVT fluid, differential oil, and warranty work to the pros.

## Verdict: The Cheapest Way to Seat Seven

If you need room for the whole family without a diesel-sized service bill, the Avanza is hard to beat — small-car PMS costs, a timing chain (no belt drama), and parts on every corner. It's **still on sale new in 2026** (1.3 J/E and 1.5 G, FWD), so casa and parts support aren't going anywhere. Keep to the 5,000 km schedule, budget for the 40K/80K heavy PMS, settle any open recall, and keep your records clean.
