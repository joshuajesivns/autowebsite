The **Toyota Avanza** is the budget 7-seater that lets a whole family move on a small-car maintenance bill. It's gas, not diesel, so servicing it is closer to a Vios than an Innova — mura at simple. Here's exactly what an Avanza costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the one thing to know about which generation you own. This is the Avanza companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Avanza runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A regular Avanza PMS at casa is about **₱5,000–7,500**; a major 40K–80K service is **₱10,000–15,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- It's **one of the cheapest 7-seaters to maintain in PH** — gas engine, cheap parts, kahit aling talyer kaya.
- **Know your generation:** pre-2022 is RWD with a 4-speed AT; 2022+ 1.5 is FWD with a CVT — different fluids to watch.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Toyota Avanza PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Avanza) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱5,000–7,500 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–15,000+ |

The Avanza sits near the small-sedan range — a modest gas engine means a light change-oil PMS. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear Items on an Avanza

Tough and simple, pero may konting bagay pa ring babantayan:

- **CVT fluid** (2022+ FWD CVT) — change it ~every 40,000 km for long-term smoothness.
- **Rear differential & transmission oil** (pre-2022 RWD) — an extra fluid the FWD models don't have; check it periodically.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **Suspension & bushings** — a loaded 7-seater on PH roads works the pang-ilalim hard. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — a full cabin in PH heat means the system gets a workout; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Avanza

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work.

Like the Vios, the Avanza's superpower is **parts availability** — walang shop sa Pilipinas na hindi marunong mag-Avanza, kaya wala kang sourcing o bargaining problem.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave CVT fluid, differential oil, and warranty work to the pros.

## Verdict: The Cheapest Way to Seat Seven

If you need room for the whole family without a diesel-sized service bill, the Avanza is hard to beat — small-car PMS costs, a timing chain (no belt drama), and parts on every corner. Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
