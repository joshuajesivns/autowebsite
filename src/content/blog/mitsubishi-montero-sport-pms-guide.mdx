The **Mitsubishi Montero Sport** is the Fortuner's biggest rival — a stylish, comfortable midsize diesel SUV with a smooth 8-speed automatic and a strong following among Filipino families. It costs more to service than an MPV, pero matibay ito at kasing-kompetitibo ng Fortuner sa araw-araw na gastos. Here's exactly what a Montero Sport costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the diesel items and known issues to watch. This is the Montero Sport companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- Mitsubishi's schedule runs the Montero Sport on a **10,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle — in PH traffic, the 6-month mark usually comes first.
- A light/standard Montero Sport PMS at casa is about **₱8,000–12,000**; a **heavy/major** 40K–80K service is **₱18,000–28,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Mitsubishi dealer.)*
- The **2.4L 4N15 diesel** (179 hp / 430 Nm) takes pricier low-ash diesel oil than a gas vehicle — the main reason PMS costs more.
- **Fuel filter, injectors, clean diesel, low-ash (DL-1) oil, and DPF health** are the items that keep it healthy long-term.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Mitsubishi Montero Sport Quick Specs

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 2.4L (2,442cc) 4N15 MIVEC 4-cyl turbo-diesel (Euro 4) |
| Power | 179 hp (181 PS) / 430 Nm @ 2,500 rpm |
| Oil capacity | ~6.5 liters with filter, low-SAPS diesel oil (5W-30, DL-1) |
| Transmission | 8-speed automatic, or 6-speed manual (base GLX 2WD) |
| Emissions | DPF (diesel particulate filter) equipped |
| Timing | Chain (no scheduled belt change) |
| PMS interval | Every 10,000 km or 6 months |

*Low-SAPS (DL-1) oil isn't optional on the 4N15 — it protects the DPF. The 8-speed automatic has been standard on this generation since its 2016 PH launch; only the base GLX 2WD uses a 6-speed manual.*

## Mitsubishi Montero Sport PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets touched. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Montero Sport diesel) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱3,000–5,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱8,000–12,000 |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱18,000–28,000+ |

Like the Fortuner, it lands well above MPV pricing — diesel oil and filters cost more, there's more oil to fill (~6.5L), and a body-on-frame SUV has more underneath to service. New-unit promos often bundle free PMS up to 20,000 km, so out-of-pocket commonly starts around the 30K service. ## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Montero Sport

Most visits are **light PMS** — diesel oil + filter and a general inspection. Mitsubishi's checksheet also calls for the **diesel fuel filter every 20,000 km** — crucial on a diesel, as it shields the expensive injectors and high-pressure pump.

The **heavy (major) PMS** at **40,000 km and 80,000 km** is where the bill grows: the air-cleaner element and brake/clutch fluid around 40,000 km, the front and rear differential gear oil at 80,000 km, plus 8-speed transmission service, a full diesel-system check, and the suspension/bushing inspection a heavy SUV needs. Budget for these ahead of time.

## Known Issues on the Montero Sport

Address these honestly, especially on the used market:

- **The 2015–2016 "sudden acceleration" reports concern the *previous* generation.** They involved the older automatic Montero Sport, not the current 4N15. Every technical investigation — the DTI panel (with DOST and UP), Mitsubishi, and the UK's independent **Horiba Mira** — found **no mechanical or electronic defect**, attributing incidents to **pedal misapplication**. A DTI ruling did flag the pedal *layout's* ergonomics, but no SUA defect was ever proven. It's fair to research; it was never established as a vehicle fault.
- **EGR / intake-manifold soot clogging.** Like most modern turbo-diesels, the 4N15 can build oily soot in the intake over time (rough running, check-engine light, reduced power). Highway runs and on-time servicing help; a periodic intake clean may be needed at higher mileage.
- **DPF clogging + oil dilution on short trips.** Pure slow city use stops the DPF from regenerating and can dilute the engine oil with fuel (watch for a *rising* oil level). Give it regular highway running and use the correct low-ash oil.
- **Heavier running costs than a gas SUV** — expected for a diesel of this size; budget accordingly.

## Casa vs. Talyer for a Montero Sport

**Under warranty → Mitsubishi casa** (keep every receipt). **Out of warranty → a trusted diesel-savvy talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work — basta tama ang low-ash (DL-1) diesel oil at parts.

Mitsubishi's network and parts availability in PH are strong, and the Montero Sport is common enough that diesel-experienced shops know it well.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, eyeball your oil level, and swap the cabin filter. Leave injector service, fuel-filter and DPF-related work, transmission service, and anything warranty-related to the pros.

## Verdict: A Stylish, Capable Alternative to the Fortuner

If you want a comfortable, good-looking midsize diesel SUV that rivals the segment leader, the Montero Sport delivers — competitive diesel PMS costs, a smooth 8-speed auto, and a timing chain (no belt drama). In 2026 it spans roughly **₱1.57M (GLX 2WD MT)** to **₱2.53M (Black Series 4WD)** — and its entry price undercuts the Fortuner's. Just respect the schedule, keep it on clean diesel, and keep your records clean.
