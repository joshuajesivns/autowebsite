The **BYD Seagull** is the small, affordable EV hatchback that became BYD Philippines' biggest single-model seller — **4,608 units in 2025 alone**. It launched here in **September 2024** and has stayed on sale continuously through 2025 and into 2026 with almost no changes, which makes it one of the easier BYD models to research: whether you're looking at a 2024, 2025, or 2026 unit, this guide covers it. It's the Seagull companion to our [BYD sales ranking piece](/blog/top-selling-byd-cars-philippines-guide), our [BYD Seal 5 DM-i guide](/blog/byd-seal-5-pms-guide), and our [general PMS cost guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Seagull is a **fully electric (BEV)** city hatchback — no engine, no PMS oil changes, distributed by **ACMobility** through BYD Cars Philippines.
- Sold as a **single GLX variant** since its **September 2024** launch — no separate trim levels, unlike the Seal 5's Dynamic/Premium split.
- **Same core specs across the 2024, 2025, and 2026 model years**: 30 kWh Blade (LFP) battery, ~75 PS / 135 Nm motor, ~300 km NEDC range.
- **Price has moved since launch**: introductory ₱898,000 in 2024, now ₱968,000 per BYD PH's own site as of mid-2026 — budget for the current figure, not the launch-year headline price.
- Warranty: **6-year/150,000 km** vehicle, **8-year/150,000 km** motor, **8-year/160,000 km** battery.
- **No PH-specific recall.** A 2024 China-only recall (16,666 units, a camera software fix) predates the PH launch and doesn't apply here.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2024–2026)

Whether you searched for "BYD Seagull 2024," "2025," or "2026," you're looking at the same car. Since its **September 27, 2024** launch, the Seagull has been sold in the Philippines as a **single GLX variant with no facelift or spec revision** — the battery, motor, and range figures below have held steady across all three model years we found on sale. The one thing that's actually changed year to year is **price**: it opened at an introductory **₱898,000**, and BYD PH's own site lists it at **₱968,000** as of May 2026. Third-party listings show figures in between (₱938,000–₱958,000) reflecting promo periods at different points — treat ₱968,000 as the current baseline and confirm the day's price with your dealer, since BYD PH runs frequent limited-time offers.

## Maintenance & PMS: What's Verified, What Isn't

Being a BEV, the Seagull skips engine oil, spark plugs, timing components, and most of what makes a gas car's PMS list long. **What we could confirm:** BYD's general EV maintenance cadence in the Philippines is **once a year or every 20,000 km**, whichever comes first — a single annual visit rather than the 5,000–10,000 km intervals gas cars need.

**What we could not confirm from an official, Seagull-specific BYD PH document:** an exact PMS peso price list. Owner reports and BYD's broader messaging both point to the Seagull sharing its Atto 3 sibling's maintenance pricing tier — BYD's own published schedule puts the Atto 3 at roughly **₱21,271.73 cumulative over 5 years (~₱4,254/year)**. Treat that as a reasonable proxy, not a confirmed Seagull figure, until your service advisor puts the exact number in writing.

## Warranty Coverage

BYD Philippines backs the Seagull with three separate clocks: **6 years or 150,000 km** on the vehicle overall, **8 years or 150,000 km** on the electric motor, and **8 years or 160,000 km** specifically on the Blade (LFP) battery pack. That battery-specific term matters most for resale value and long-term ownership confidence — it's the component buyers worry about most on any EV.

## Known Issues & Recall Status

The Seagull has had roughly a year and a half on Philippine roads as of mid-2026 — more real-world mileage than newer BYD models like the Seal 5, but still short of a long-term track record. Here's what we found:

- **No PH-specific recall.** BYD did recall 16,666 Seagull units in **China** in 2024 over a reverse-camera display software bug — but those units were built **April–May 2023**, well before the Seagull even launched in the Philippines, and the fix was an OTA software update. It has no bearing on PH-market cars.
- **No widespread mechanical defect pattern** has surfaced in PH owner reviews or forums so far.
- As with any EV, expect the usual wear items to matter more than engine parts: **tire wear** (instant torque plus battery weight can wear front tires faster than a comparable gas hatchback), **12V auxiliary battery health**, and **brake fluid** (regenerative braking extends pad life, but fluid still ages on a calendar schedule regardless).
- Being a small city car, cargo space and rear-seat room are tight by design — not a defect, just a trade-off worth knowing before you buy.

## Dealer Network & Service Access

BYD's PH dealer network scaled from **2 outlets in early 2024 to 79 by February 2026**, so service access has improved substantially since the Seagull's launch — but coverage is still newer and thinner than legacy brands. As with any BYD EV, **high-voltage battery and motor work should stay with an authorized BYD service center**, not an independent talyer.

## What You Can DIY

Tire pressure, washer fluid, cabin filter swaps, and wiper blades are all fine to handle yourself. Leave the high-voltage system, drive motor, and battery management diagnostics to BYD-trained technicians — the savings from a generic shop aren't worth the risk on an EV's electrical architecture.

## Verdict

The Seagull earned its best-seller status honestly: it's the cheapest real entry point into BYD's EV lineup, its spec sheet hasn't changed since 2024 (so buying a slightly older unit isn't a downgrade), and its once-a-year PMS cadence is genuinely lighter than any gas-powered rival at this price point. The only real homework is on pricing — confirm the current SRP and any active promo before you commit, since the headline price has moved a fair amount since the 2024 launch.
