The **Nissan Almera** is the value play in the subcompact sedan class — roomy, well-equipped, and (in its current form) running a punchy little 1.0L turbo. Maintaining it is affordable and close to its rivals, pero may isang bagay ang turbo na hindi mo dapat kalimutan: oil discipline. Here's exactly what an Almera costs to service, interval by interval, plus the items to watch. This is the Almera companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Almera runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A regular Almera PMS at casa is about **₱5,000–7,500**; a major 40K–80K service is **₱10,000–15,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Nissan dealer.)*
- **Turbo discipline (1.0L, 2020+):** use quality oil and never stretch the interval — turbos are less forgiving of dirty oil than NA engines.
- The older **1.5L (2012–2019)** is naturally aspirated and even more relaxed about oil.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Nissan Almera PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Almera) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱5,000–7,500 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–15,000+ |

The Almera sits squarely in the subcompact-sedan range. The turbo doesn't make routine PMS dramatically pricier — it just raises the stakes on doing it *on time* with the right oil. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear Items on an Almera

A solid car, pero may babantayan — lalo na sa turbo:

- **Engine oil (turbo)** — quality oil, changed on schedule, is the single most important thing for the 1.0L turbo's longevity.
- **CVT fluid** — many owners change it ~every 40,000 km for long-term smoothness, kahit 'inspect' lang ang sabi ng manual.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **Pang-ilalim** — bushings, tie rod ends, and stabilizer links take a beating on PH roads. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — PH heat works the system hard; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Almera

**Under warranty → Nissan casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work — but for the turbo, make sure the shop uses the correct oil spec.

One honest note: Nissan's dealer network and parts availability aren't quite as dense as Toyota's, so a casa visit can be a touch less convenient in some areas. Plan ahead for parts on the turbo model.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave CVT fluid, turbo-related work, and warranty service to the pros.

## Verdict: Great Value If You Respect the Oil

The Almera gives you more space and a peppier turbo for the money than most rivals, with affordable routine PMS and a timing chain (no belt drama). The deal: treat oil changes as non-negotiable on the turbo, and keep your records clean.
