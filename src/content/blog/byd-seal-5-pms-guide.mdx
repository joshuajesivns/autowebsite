The **BYD Seal 5 DM-i** is BYD's push into the compact PHEV sedan space — Civic/Corolla-sized, but with a plug and over **1,000 km of combined range**. It launched in the Philippines in **November 2024** in two trims, **Dynamic** and **Premium**, distributed by **ACMobility** through BYD Cars Philippines. Because it's still a young model here, we're being upfront about what's actually verified versus what still needs a dealer confirmation. This is the Seal 5 companion to our [BYD sales ranking piece](/blog/top-selling-byd-cars-philippines-guide) and our [general PMS cost guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Seal 5 DM-i is a **PHEV** (plug-in hybrid) — a 1.5L engine works as a generator alongside an electric motor, using BYD's **Super DM-i** system and **Blade (LFP) battery**.
- **Dynamic** (~₱948,000–958,000): 8.3 kWh battery, ~50 km EV range, ~179 PS combined.
- **Premium** (~₱1,198,000): 18.3 kWh battery, ~115 km EV range, ~194–197 PS combined, plus a longer feature list (dual-zone AC, sunroof, 360° camera, V2L).
- Standard BYD PH coverage: **6-year vehicle warranty, 8-year battery warranty** — long enough that PMS cost matters less than most gas cars.
- **No official BYD PH PMS price sheet was found** for this exact model — informally reported intervals exist but aren't confirmed; verify with your dealer.
- Too new for a reliable "known issues" list — we're saying so honestly rather than guessing.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2024–2026)

This guide covers the BYD Seal 5 DM-i since its Philippine launch in **November 2024** through 2026 — both the **Dynamic** and **Premium** trims, which launched together and share the same generation and platform. There's no older or newer Seal 5 DM-i generation to distinguish here yet; the trim differences covered above (battery size, EV range, feature list) are the only major variation within this model.

## Dynamic vs. Premium: What You Actually Get

The two trims aren't just a features gap — the **powertrain itself differs**, with the Premium carrying more than double the battery capacity and noticeably longer EV-only range. On top of that, Premium adds:

- **17" wheels** (vs. 16" on Dynamic)
- **Dual-zone automatic AC** (vs. single-zone)
- **Power-adjustable driver's seat** (vs. manual 6-way)
- **Sunroof, power-folding mirrors, puddle lamps**
- **8-speaker audio** (vs. 6)
- **360° camera with see-through mode** and extra front radars (vs. rear camera only)
- **V2L (Vehicle-to-Load) with a bundled 7kW charger** — lets the car power external devices/appliances

If your daily driving fits inside ~50 km, the Dynamic covers most trips on electric power alone and saves roughly ₱240,000–250,000. The Premium is the pick if you want meaningfully more EV-only range and the fuller feature set.

## Maintenance & PMS: What's Verified, What Isn't

Because a PHEV still carries a combustion engine for the "generator" side, it still needs conventional PMS-style servicing — oil changes, filters, brake checks — on top of whatever's specific to the hybrid/EV system.

**What we could confirm:** BYD Philippines backs the Seal 5 with a standard **6-year vehicle warranty** and an **8-year warranty on the Blade (LFP) battery**, the same policy applied across BYD's PH lineup.

**What we could not confirm from an official BYD PH source:** an exact, published PMS price list specific to the Seal 5. Some secondary sources describe a schedule of a free first service at **1,000 km / 1 month**, a second at **3,000 km / 3 months**, then **every 20,000 km / 1 year** after — but we could not trace this to an official BYD PH document, so treat it as unverified until your dealer confirms it in writing. We also could not find a published oil type/capacity spec for the 1.5L generator engine — get this from your owner's manual or service advisor rather than guessing.

## Known Issues on the BYD Seal 5

Being honest here: the Seal 5 DM-i has only been on Philippine roads since late 2024/early 2025, so there isn't enough mileage or ownership data yet for a real "known issues" list. What we can say:

- **No PH-specific recall** has been issued for the Seal 5. (A 2025 global BYD recall affecting 115,000+ units applied to older Tang and Yuan Pro EVs — unrelated models, and ACMobility has stated it does not affect PH-market units.)
- The small sample of PH owner reviews available so far is **positive with no reported defects**, but the sample size is too small to draw conclusions from.
- As with any PHEV, expect the usual EV-adjacent maintenance items over time — 12V battery health, brake fluid (regenerative braking can extend brake-pad life but fluid still ages on schedule), and tire wear (instant torque and battery weight can wear front tires faster than a comparable gas sedan).

We'll update this guide as more Seal 5 units rack up real-world mileage in the Philippines.

## Dealer Network & Service Access

BYD's PH dealer network expanded fast — from just **2 outlets in early 2024** to **79 by February 2026** — which matters for a PHEV where high-voltage system work should stay with an authorized BYD service center rather than an independent talyer. Coverage is growing quickly but is still newer and less dense than legacy brands like Toyota or Mitsubishi; confirm service availability in your area before buying.

## What You Can DIY

Tire pressure, washer fluid, cabin filter, and basic visual checks are fine to handle yourself. **Leave anything touching the high-voltage battery, drive motor, or hybrid control system to BYD-trained technicians** — this isn't a car to have "diagnosed" at a generic talyer the way you might a Vios or Brio.

## Verdict: A Strong Case, With Some Verification Homework

The Seal 5 DM-i is a genuinely compelling proposition on paper — PHEV flexibility, a long combined range, and a 6-year/8-year warranty that reduces near-term maintenance risk. The catch is that it's still building a track record in the Philippines: get your dealer to put the PMS schedule and pricing in writing before you sign, and go in with realistic expectations about how new this ownership experience still is.
