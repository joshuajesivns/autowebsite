The **Toyota Innova** is the workhorse family MPV of the Philippines — diesel, durable, and built to swallow seven or eight people plus their bags without complaint. The current Philippine Innova is **diesel-only**, powered by the 2.8L 1GD-FTV turbo-diesel; the newer monocoque hybrid is sold here as a *separate* model, the **Toyota Zenix**. It costs a little more to service than a small sedan, pero kapalit nito ay tibay na halos walang katulad. Here's exactly what an Innova costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the diesel-specific items to watch. This is the Innova companion to our [in-depth car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Innova runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle — and with a diesel, sticking to it matters even more.
- A light/standard PMS runs roughly **₱3,000–8,700** by interval; the **heavy PMS** at 80,000 km can reach **₱11,700–21,700**. *(Average casa figures, June 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- The **2.8L diesel** takes ~7–7.5L of pricier diesel-spec oil — the main reason PMS costs more than a gas sedan.
- **Fuel filter, injectors, clean diesel, and the DPF** are the items that keep a diesel Innova healthy long-term.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later, plus **strong resale** for an MPV.

## Toyota Innova Quick Specs

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 2.8L (2,755cc) 1GD-FTV 4-cylinder turbo-diesel |
| Power | 172 hp / 360 Nm (AT) · 168 hp / 343 Nm (MT) |
| Oil capacity | ~7–7.5 liters, diesel-spec (e.g. 0W-30) |

| Emissions | DPF (diesel particulate filter) equipped |
| Timing | Chain (no scheduled belt change) |
| PMS interval | Every 5,000 km or 6 months |

*The current PH Innova is diesel-only. The hybrid monocoque MPV is sold separately as the Toyota Zenix.*

## Toyota Innova PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets touched. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Innova diesel) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱1,900–2,300 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱3,000–8,700 |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱7,800–21,700 *(80K is the costliest)* |

The Innova lands a notch above the small-sedan range because diesel oil and filters cost more and there's simply more oil to fill (~7–7.5L vs ~3.5L on a sedan). The 80,000 km service is the heaviest — fuel filter, fluids, and a deep check. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [major (heavy) PMS cost breakdown](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Innova

Most Innova visits are **light PMS** — diesel oil + filter and a general inspection, every 5,000 km. They cost more than a sedan's only because of the oil volume.

The **heavy (major) PMS** at **40,000 km and 80,000 km** is where the bill grows: the **fuel filter and water separator**, brake fluid, coolant, transmission fluid, and a full diesel-system check. The 80,000 km service is the costliest of the lot, so budget for it ahead of time.

## Known Issues on the Toyota Innova

The Innova is bulletproof, pero may diesel-specific na babantayan:

- **Fuel-pump recall (2017–2019 units).** Toyota Philippines recalled Innova units built Jan 2017–May 2019 for a Denso fuel pump that could deform and cause stalling — the fix is a free replacement. If you're buying used, confirm it was done.
- **DPF clogging from short trips.** The diesel particulate filter needs longer, hotter drives to burn off soot. Puro maikli't matrapik na biyahe? It may clog and trigger a warning — give it a regular highway run to let it regenerate.
- **Early oil consumption / dilution.** Some early 1GD units used oil or showed fuel-in-oil dilution; Toyota addressed it with an ECU update. Keep oil changes on time and watch your level.
- **Pang-ilalim under load.** A fully loaded MPV on PH roads works the suspension and bushings hard. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` over bumps — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon.** A packed cabin in PH heat makes the system work overtime; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Innova

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (keep every receipt). **Out of warranty → a trusted diesel-savvy talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work — basta tama ang diesel-spec oil at parts.

The Innova's advantage is ubiquity: it's everywhere in fleets and families, kaya alam ng halos lahat ng shop kung paano ito ayusin, and parts are easy to source at fair prices.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure (important on a heavy MPV), top up washer fluid, eyeball your oil level, and swap the cabin filter. Leave injector service, fuel-filter changes, and anything warranty-related to the pros.

## Verdict: The Sensible Family Diesel

If you need to move people and cargo cheaply for years, the Innova is hard to beat — modest diesel PMS bills for its size, a timing chain (no belt drama), parts everywhere, and strong resale in the MPV class. It's **still on sale new in 2026** (J, XE, E, G, and V, ₱1.27M–₱1.81M). Just respect the schedule, mind the DPF and fuel filter, and keep your records clean.
