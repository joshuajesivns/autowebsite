The **Toyota Innova** is the workhorse family MPV of the Philippines — diesel, durable, and built to swallow seven or eight people plus their bags without complaint. It costs a little more to service than a small sedan, pero kapalit nito ay tibay na halos walang katulad. Here's exactly what an Innova costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the diesel-specific items to watch. This is the Innova companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Innova runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle — and with a diesel, sticking to it matters even more.
- A regular Innova PMS at casa is about **₱7,000–10,000**; a major 40K–80K service is **₱15,000–25,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- The **2.8L diesel** takes more oil (~7.5L) and pricier diesel-spec oil than a gas sedan — that's the main reason PMS costs more.
- **Fuel filter, injectors, and clean diesel** are the items that keep a diesel Innova healthy long-term.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later, and **best-in-class resale** for an MPV.

## Toyota Innova PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets touched. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Innova diesel) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱3,000–5,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱7,000–10,000 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱15,000–25,000+ |

The Innova lands a notch above the small-sedan range because diesel oil and filters cost more and there's simply more oil to fill. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear & Diesel Items on an Innova

The Innova is bulletproof, pero may diesel-specific na babantayan:

- **Fuel filter** — replace on schedule (or sooner with dirty diesel); it shields the expensive injectors and fuel pump.
- **Engine oil** — use the correct diesel-spec oil and don't stretch the interval; diesels are unforgiving of dirty oil.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **Suspension & bushings** — a fully loaded MPV on PH roads works the pang-ilalim hard. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` over bumps — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — a packed cabin in PH heat makes the system work overtime; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Innova

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (keep every receipt). **Out of warranty → a trusted diesel-savvy talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work — basta tama ang diesel-spec oil at parts.

The Innova's advantage is ubiquity: it's everywhere in fleets and families, kaya alam ng halos lahat ng shop kung paano ito ayusin, and parts are easy to source at fair prices.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure (important on a heavy MPV), top up washer fluid, eyeball your oil level, and swap the cabin filter. Leave injector service, fuel-filter changes, and anything warranty-related to the pros.

## Verdict: The Sensible Family Diesel

If you need to move people and cargo cheaply for years, the Innova is hard to beat — modest diesel PMS bills for its size, a timing chain (no belt drama), parts everywhere, and the strongest resale in the MPV class. Just respect the schedule and keep your records clean.
