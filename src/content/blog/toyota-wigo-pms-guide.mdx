The **Toyota Wigo** is the entry point to Toyota ownership — a 1.0L three-cylinder city car that's about as cheap to run as a car gets in the Philippines. Tiny engine, tiny oil change, Toyota parts everywhere. The current second-generation Wigo (launched July 2023) keeps the proven **1.0-liter 1KR-VE** three-cylinder — now with Dual VVT-i, good for **67 PS and 89 Nm** — on Daihatsu's newer DNGA platform. Here's the PMS breakdown. This is the Wigo companion to our [full guide to car maintenance in the Philippines](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- Runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- **About the cheapest car to service in PH** — a light/standard PMS sits at the **low end of ₱5,000–7,000** (often less). *(Average casa figures, June 2026 — verify with your dealer.)*
- The **heavy / major PMS** (40K & 80K km) is the big-bill stop — Toyota's published **40,000 km package is about ₱13,000+**, roughly 3× a light PMS.
- 1.0L engine needs only **~2.7L of oil** (0W-20) — featherweight change-oil bills.
- **Toyota parts + network everywhere**, plus a **timing chain** (no belt to replace).
- New gen offers a **CVT** (E, G) or 5-speed manual (J); the old 2014–2022 car used a 4-speed automatic.
- Great resale for a city car if you keep the records clean.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2023–2026, 2nd-gen)

This guide covers the **current 2nd-generation Wigo**, launched in the Philippines in **July 2023** on Daihatsu's newer DNGA-A platform — no facelift since, so everything here applies whether you're looking at a 2023, 2024, or 2026 unit. The previous-generation Wigo (2014–2022, including a 2020 cosmetic refresh) ran the older 1KR-FE engine with a 4-speed automatic instead of a CVT, so its PMS specifics differ enough to fall outside the scope of this guide.

## Toyota Wigo Quick Specs

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 1.0L (998cc) 1KR-VE 3-cylinder DOHC, Dual VVT-i |
| Power | 67 PS @ 6,000 rpm / 89 Nm @ 4,400 rpm |
| Oil capacity | ~2.7 liters (with filter), 0W-20 |
| Transmission | 5-speed manual (J) or CVT (E, G) |
| Timing | Chain (no scheduled belt change) |
| PMS interval | Every 5,000 km or 6 months |

*Figures for the current 2nd-gen (2023–present) PH Wigo. The 2014–2022 model used the 1KR-FE (65 PS) with a 4-speed automatic.*

## Toyota Wigo PMS Cost by Interval

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Wigo) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱3,500–7,000 *(often the low end)* |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱13,000+ |

The Wigo is at the **rock-bottom end** of the range — the 1.0L sips just ~2.7L of oil, so light PMS is cheap. The numbers above line up with Toyota Philippines' own published check-up packages: roughly **₱4,400 at 10,000 km**, **₱3,700 at 20,000 km**, and **₱13,000+ at 40,000 km**. These are the standardized per-interval menus a 1.0L car falls under — your exact bill still varies by variant and dealer, so for a model-specific figure use Toyota's **maintenance cost calculator** at toyota.com.ph. For what's done at each interval, see the [light-vs-heavy PMS explainer](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Wigo

Most Wigo visits are **light PMS** — engine oil + filter, tire rotation, and a general check, done every 5,000 km. They're quick and cheap precisely because the 1.0L needs so little oil.

The **heavy (major) PMS** lands at **40,000 km and 80,000 km**. On top of the oil change, the casa replaces the items that only come due occasionally — air and fuel filters, brake fluid, the CVT/automatic fluid, and spark plugs. That's why the 40K bill jumps to ~₱13,000+ while a light PMS stays under ₱7,000. Budget for the 40K and 80K stops in advance para hindi ka ma-shock.

## Known Issues on the Toyota Wigo

No car is perfect, even a cheap-to-run one. The recurring, owner-reported notes on the Wigo:

- **Three-cylinder vibration at idle.** Inherent to a 1.0L triple — you'll feel a light shake, lalo na sa stoplight. More noticeable on the older 2014–2022 car; the 2nd gen damped it down but didn't erase it.
- **Engine mounts soften over time.** That same three-cylinder buzz works the mounts, and worn mounts make the vibration worse. A common, not-expensive fix after a few years — worth checking if the shake grows.
- **Cabin noise (NVH).** This is a budget city car: expect road, wind, and some powertrain/CVT noise into the cabin, especially at highway speed. The 2nd gen is quieter than the old one, pero hindi pa rin library-tahimik.
- **Pang-ilalim wear on rough roads.** Light car, but bushings and links still take a beating on PH roads — listen for `lagutok` or `kalog` over bumps. We decode what those specific noises usually mean in our [suspension-noise troubleshooting rundown](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

None of these are deal-breakers — they're the normal trade-offs of the cheapest Toyota you can buy, and all are cheap to address.

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

Under warranty → **Toyota casa**. After → any talyer, kasi simple at common ang engine. Parts are everywhere and cheap. If you're weighing it against a slightly bigger first car, compare with the [Vios PMS guide](/blog/toyota-vios-pms-guide).

## Verdict

For sheer low running cost, the Wigo is near the top of the list — minimal light-PMS bills, Toyota's parts network behind it, and a timing chain. It's **still on sale new in 2026** (1.0 J manual, 1.0 E and 1.0 G CVT), so parts and casa support aren't going anywhere. Keep to the schedule, budget for the 40K/80K heavy PMS, keep your records, and it's one of the most worry-free, wallet-friendly cars you can own in the PH.
