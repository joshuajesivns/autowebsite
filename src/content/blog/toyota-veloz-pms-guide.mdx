The **Toyota Veloz** is the premium 7-seater that gives a growing family SUV-style looks and a proper features list — Toyota Safety Sense, six airbags — on a small-car maintenance bill. Under the skin it's the Avanza's more upscale sibling: same **DNGA platform**, same **1.5L gas engine**, so servicing it is mura at simple, closer to a Vios than to a diesel MPV. Here's exactly what a Veloz costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the PMS schedule, the timing chain answer, the recalls, and the known issues to watch. This is the Veloz companion to our [full guide to PMS meaning, schedule and cost](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- **PMS schedule:** the Veloz runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** cycle — oil + filter each light PMS, with heavy/major service at **40,000 km and 80,000 km**. Full chart below.
- A **light/standard PMS** at casa is about **₱3,600–5,200**; the first PMS is **₱1,500–2,200**. *(Average casa estimates, July 2026 — verify with your Toyota dealer.)*
- The **heavy/major PMS** (40K & 80K km) is the big-bill stop — roughly **₱7,500–9,000 at 40,000 km** and **₱11,500–13,500 at 80,000 km**.
- **Same engine as the Avanza 1.5** (2NR-VE) — so oil, filters, and PMS logic are identical; the Veloz is just CVT-only and more premium.
- **Timing belt or chain?** **Chain** — no scheduled belt replacement later.
- **Three PH recalls exist** (brakes, side-impact beam, shock absorbers) — all free; confirm a used unit has had them done.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2022–2026)

This guide covers the Toyota Veloz since its Philippine launch on **April 29, 2022** — the point at which the Veloz stopped being the Avanza's top trim and became its own standalone nameplate. It's the only generation sold here to date, spanning the 2022 through 2026 model years with no structural change, just trim and price adjustments. The powertrain — the **1.5L 2NR-VE** with a **D-CVT** — has been the same across that entire span, so the maintenance information here applies whether you're shopping a 2022 unit or a brand-new 2026 one. If you remember an older "Avanza Veloz" badge from before 2022, that was the previous-generation Avanza's top variant, not this DNGA-based standalone model.

| Spec | Detail |

| Power | 105–106 PS / 138 Nm |

## Toyota Veloz Maintenance Schedule (PMS Interval Chart)

The Veloz follows Toyota's standard **every 5,000 km or 6 months** PMS cadence — oil and filter plus an inspection at every stop, with the bigger-ticket items landing at the 40,000 km and 80,000 km "heavy" services. Here's the full schedule of *what actually gets done* at each interval:

| Odometer (km) | PMS type | Key items done |
|---|---|---|
| 1,000 | First check (free) | First inspection, oil level + bolt-torque check, no major parts |
| 5,000 | Light | Engine oil + oil filter, general inspection |
| 10,000 | Light | Oil + filter, tire rotation, brake inspection |
| 15,000 | Light | Oil + filter, general inspection |
| 20,000 | Light | Oil + filter, air-cleaner element check, fuel-system check |
| 30,000 | Light | Oil + filter, brake + suspension inspection |
| **40,000** | **Heavy / major** | Oil + filter, **brake fluid, coolant, spark plugs, air + fuel filters**, full inspection |
| 50,000–70,000 | Light | Oil + filter each 5k/6-mo stop, rotating inspections |
| **80,000** | **Heavy / major** | Everything at 40K **+ CVT fluid** (dedicated Toyota fluid), suspension + bushing inspection |

*Whichever comes first — km or months. For loaded-family PH use (seven aboard, traffic, heat), Toyota treats the schedule as "severe," so don't stretch the oil interval.*

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Veloz

Most Veloz visits are **light PMS** — engine oil + filter, tire rotation, and a general check, every 5,000 km. They're quick and cheap because the 1.5L only sips about 3.3L of oil.

The **heavy (major) PMS** lands at **40,000 km and 80,000 km**. On top of the oil change, the casa replaces the items that only come due occasionally — air and fuel filters, brake fluid, coolant, spark plugs, and — at 80,000 km — the **CVT fluid**. That's why the 40K bill roughly doubles a light PMS, and 80K climbs higher still. The CVT uses a dedicated Toyota fluid, not generic ATF; the wrong fluid is a real damage risk, so leave it to the casa. Budget for the 40K and 80K stops in advance para hindi ka ma-shock.

## Toyota Veloz: Timing Belt or Chain?

**Chain.** The 1.5L 2NR-VE uses a **timing chain**, not a rubber timing belt — same as the Avanza and the rest of Toyota's NR engine family. That means there's **no scheduled timing-belt replacement** (the ₱15,000–30,000+ job belt-driven cars need around 100,000 km). A chain is built to last the engine's life on clean oil, which is one more reason to keep the oil changes on time.

## Toyota Veloz CVT Fluid Change Interval

Toyota doesn't print the CVT fluid as a fixed every-visit line item, but in practice it's inspected and replaced around the **80,000 km** heavy PMS — **earlier (roughly every 40,000 km)** if you're always in severe, heavy-traffic driving. The Veloz's **D-CVT** takes a **dedicated Toyota CVT fluid**, never generic ATF — the wrong fluid is an immediate damage risk, so this one belongs at the casa. *(Interval is average casa practice, July 2026 — confirm with your dealer for your exact variant.)*

## Known Issues on the Toyota Veloz

No car is perfect, even a cheap-to-run one. The recurring, owner- and reviewer-reported notes on the Veloz:

- **Underpowered when fully loaded.** The 1.5L feels strained with seven aboard plus aircon, and the **CVT can feel hesitant under load and on uphills**. Reviewers treat this as a characteristic of a small-engined 7-seater, *not* a failure — there are no widespread CVT-failure reports on the Veloz.
- **Cabin noise / NVH.** Owners note road and tire noise at highway speed; the engine hood insulation can come loose and vibrate on rough roads, and the rear doors could use more sound sealing. There's also no under-engine cover from the factory.
- **Single-mode front airflow.** A documented owner gripe: the HVAC directs air to the front with limited separate windshield/lower-vent options — a comfort quirk, not a fault.
- **Suspension & bushings under load.** A loaded 7-seater works the pang-ilalim hard on PH roads — listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` over bumps. Full sound guide in our [broader PMS resource for PH drivers](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Check for open recalls.** The Veloz has **three PH recalls**, all free at Toyota: a **brake cylinder slide-pin boot** issue (Jan 2024, 3,511 units built Sep–Dec 2022, where water ingress could rust the pin and wear pads early), a **front side-impact beam welding** recall (shared with the Avanza), and a **front shock-absorber nut** recall (shared with the Avanza and Yaris Cross, where a loose nut can cause abnormal noise). Confirm your VIN with the dealer.

None of these are deal-breakers — they're the normal trade-offs of an affordable 7-seater, and the recalls are free to resolve.

## Toyota Veloz Warranty — and Why On-Time PMS Pays

Every new Veloz comes with Toyota Motor Philippines' standard **3-year / 100,000 km** manufacturer warranty. Since January 2025, Toyota PH has also offered a **Service Loyalty Warranty** that can stretch coverage to **up to 5 years / an extra 40,000 km** — *but only if you keep your PMS at a Toyota dealer* (at least one PMS per year through the warranty period, six visits in the first three years). In other words, sticking to the casa schedule during the warranty years isn't just good hygiene — it's what unlocks the extra two years of coverage. Keep every PMS receipt.

*(Standard warranty is 3 yr/100,000 km; the loyalty extension adds up to 2 yr/40,000 km for units sold January 1, 2025 onward, subject to Toyota's PMS conditions — confirm current terms with your dealer.)*

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

Easy call: **under warranty → Toyota casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work — but for the CVT, make sure the shop uses the correct Toyota fluid. Like the Avanza, the Veloz's superpower is **parts availability** — it shares its engine and much of its hardware with one of the country's best-selling MPVs, so walang sourcing o bargaining problem. Between visits you can DIY the basics: tire pressure, washer fluid, cabin filter, and an oil-level eyeball; leave CVT fluid and warranty/recall work to the pros.

## Verdict: Premium 7-Seater, Small-Car Running Cost

If you want a family hauler that feels a class above the basics but still services like a small gas car, the Veloz is a strong pick — small-MPV PMS costs, a timing chain (no belt drama), and Avanza-shared parts on every corner. The deal: keep to the 5,000 km schedule, budget for the 40K/80K heavy PMS, settle any of the three open recalls, and keep your records clean.
