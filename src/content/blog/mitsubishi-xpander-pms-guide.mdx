The **Mitsubishi Xpander** is the best-selling MPV in the Philippines for a simple reason: SUV-like styling and ground clearance, seven seats, and a small-car maintenance bill. It's gas, not diesel, so servicing it is closer to a Vios than a Fortuner — mura at simple. Here's exactly what an Xpander costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the items and known issues to watch. This is the Xpander companion to our [full car PMS price guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Xpander runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A light/standard Xpander PMS at casa is about **₱5,000–7,500**; a **heavy/major** 40K–80K service is **₱10,000–16,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Mitsubishi dealer.)*
- It's **one of the cheapest 7-seaters to maintain in PH** — gas engine, modest 1.5L, affordable parts.
- **No CVT here:** the PH Xpander uses a 4-speed automatic (or 5-speed manual) — the CVT is an export-market thing, so there's no CVT fluid to budget for.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Which Model Year Does This Guide Cover? (2018–2026)

This guide covers the Mitsubishi Xpander across its entire Philippine production run — a single generation that Mitsubishi Motors Philippines Corporation first launched here in March 2018, with sales beginning that May. It's been through several cosmetic refreshes since, most recently the 2026 facelift, but every model year from 2018 through 2026 shares the same drivetrain: a 1.5L 4A91 gas engine paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic. That includes the 2026 facelift — the Philippine-spec Xpander has **never** used a CVT in any model year, unlike the Xpander sold in Indonesia and Thailand. If your unit is a locally-bought 2018–2026 Xpander, everything in this guide applies to you.

## Mitsubishi Xpander Quick Specs

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 1.5L (1,499cc) 4A91 DOHC 16-valve MIVEC, naturally aspirated |
| Power | 103 hp @ 6,000 rpm / 141 Nm @ 4,000 rpm |
| Oil capacity | ~4 liters with filter (SAE 5W-30) |
| Transmission | 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic |
| Timing | Chain (no scheduled belt change) |
| PMS interval | Every 5,000 km or 6 months |

*The Philippine Xpander has only ever used a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic — including the latest 2026 facelift. The CVT sold in some export markets has never been offered here, so there's no CVT fluid interval to worry about.*

## Mitsubishi Xpander PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Xpander) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱5,000–7,500 |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–16,000+ |

The Xpander sits near the small-sedan range — a modest gas engine means a light change-oil PMS. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [how much each service interval costs](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Xpander

Most Xpander visits are **light PMS** — engine oil + filter and a general inspection, every 5,000 km. On a 1.5L gas engine that's cheap; it's the same routine as a Mirage or Vios, just on an MPV body.

The **heavy (major) PMS** at **40,000 km and 80,000 km** is where the bill grows: brake fluid, coolant, the air-cleaner element, spark plugs (around 60,000 km), automatic-transmission fluid for the 4-speed AT, plus a full inspection of the suspension and bushings that a loaded 7-seater works hard. Budget a bit more for these stops — but they're still modest next to any diesel SUV.

## Known Issues on the Xpander

Generally reliable, pero may ilang bagay na dapat malaman ng buyer:

- **Fuel-pump recall (2018–2019 units).** Mitsubishi Philippines recalled Xpander units purchased **May 2018 – October 2019** for a Denso fuel-pump component that could swell and stall the engine — the fix is a free replacement. If you're buying used, confirm it was done.
- **Modest power when fully loaded** — the naturally-aspirated 1.5L is efficient, not punchy; seven passengers plus aircon up an incline will feel leisurely. Hindi ito depekto, asahan mo lang.
- **The 4-speed automatic** — smooth and durable, but a dated 4-speed feels its age and is less efficient than the CVTs rivals use. It's reliable, basta sundin ang ATF schedule.
- **Soft rear suspension on rough roads** — owners report a bit of float and the occasional rear `kalog` when loaded over bad pavement; check the bushings on a used unit. See our [page explaining PMS costs across every vehicle type](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon in peak heat** — a large cabin in PH heat makes the system work hard; keep it serviced and expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

No verified brake or airbag recall applies to the PH Xpander — treat the items above as the documented ones; the rest of its record is the reliability that makes it a top seller.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Xpander

Easy call: **under warranty → Mitsubishi casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work.

Mitsubishi's network and parts availability in PH are strong — not quite Toyota-dense, but the Xpander is common enough that shops know it well and parts are easy to source.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave transmission fluid and warranty work to the pros.

## Verdict: The Cheapest Way to Look Like an SUV and Seat Seven

If you want SUV presence and room for the whole family without a diesel-sized service bill, the Xpander is hard to beat — small-car PMS costs, a timing chain (no belt drama), and a strong reliability record. Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
