The **Mitsubishi Xpander** is the best-selling MPV in the Philippines for a simple reason: SUV-like styling and ground clearance, seven seats, and a small-car maintenance bill. It's gas, not diesel, so servicing it is closer to a Vios than a Fortuner — mura at simple. Here's exactly what an Xpander costs to maintain, interval by interval, plus the items and known issues to watch. This is the Xpander companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Xpander runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A regular Xpander PMS at casa is about **₱5,000–7,500**; a major 40K–80K service is **₱10,000–16,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Mitsubishi dealer.)*
- It's **one of the cheapest 7-seaters to maintain in PH** — gas engine, modest 1.5L, affordable parts.
- **Know your transmission:** 2022+ is CVT (watch the CVT fluid); earlier units are a 4-speed AT.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Mitsubishi Xpander PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Xpander) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱5,000–7,500 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–16,000+ |

The Xpander sits near the small-sedan range — a modest gas engine means a light change-oil PMS. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear Items on an Xpander

Tough and simple, pero may konting bagay pa ring babantayan:

- **CVT fluid** (2022+ CVT) — change it ~every 40,000 km for long-term smoothness.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **Suspension & bushings** — a loaded 7-seater on PH roads works the pang-ilalim hard. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — a full cabin in PH heat means the system gets a workout; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Known Issues on the Xpander

Generally reliable, pero may ilang bagay na madalas itanong ng mga buyer:

- **Modest power when fully loaded** — the 1.5L is efficient, not punchy; seven passengers plus aircon up an incline will feel leisurely. Hindi ito depekto, asahan mo lang.
- **Earlier 4-speed AT** — older units use a dated 4-speed automatic; smooth and durable, but less efficient than the newer CVT. Pre-owned buyers should know which one they're getting.
- **Aircon cooling in peak heat** — some owners add tint or servicing to keep the large cabin cool; keep the system maintained.

These are characteristics to set expectations, not red flags — the Xpander's reliability record is a big part of why it sells.

## Casa vs. Talyer for an Xpander

Easy call: **under warranty → Mitsubishi casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually noticeably cheaper for the same work.

Mitsubishi's network and parts availability in PH are strong — not quite Toyota-dense, but the Xpander is common enough that shops know it well and parts are easy to source.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave CVT fluid and warranty work to the pros.

## Verdict: The Cheapest Way to Look Like an SUV and Seat Seven

If you want SUV presence and room for the whole family without a diesel-sized service bill, the Xpander is hard to beat — small-car PMS costs, a timing chain (no belt drama), and a strong reliability record. Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
