The **Suzuki Dzire** is still the quiet champion of low running costs — but the car itself is all-new. The version on sale in the Philippines today is the **4th-generation Dzire Hybrid** (launched April 2025), with a fresh **1.2L 3-cylinder mild-hybrid engine** and a CVT. It sips fuel, barely needs any oil at PMS time, and remains one of the strongest cases in the class if your priority is the lowest cost per kilometer. Here's exactly what the new Dzire costs to maintain, light versus heavy PMS, interval by interval. This is the Dzire companion to our [guide to car PMS costs in the Philippines](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Dzire generally runs the **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle — but confirm the exact interval in your service booklet, as Suzuki PH has used shorter cycles on some models.
- Suzuki PH **doesn't publish a fixed Dzire price list**, so figures are estimates: a light/standard PMS is roughly **₱2,500–5,000**, among the cheapest sedans to service; a heavy/major PMS costs more. *(Owner estimates, June 2026 — verify with your Suzuki dealer.)*
- The new **3-cylinder Z12E** takes only ~3.2L of 0W-20 oil, so a basic change-oil PMS is very light on the wallet.
- It's a **12V mild hybrid (SHVS)** — not a full hybrid; there's no large, expensive hybrid battery to worry about.
- PH variants are **CVT only** (GL and GLX) — no manual, no AGS on the current car.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Dzire

Most visits are **light PMS** — engine oil + filter, a general check, and basic top-ups. The **heavy (major) PMS** is when the casa also replaces the occasional-wear items: air and fuel filters, brake fluid, spark plugs, and the **CVT fluid** for long-term smoothness. Because the Dzire's engine is so small and oil-light, even the heavy services stay affordable by class standards — the main cost driver is the CVT-fluid and filter work, not the oil. Confirm the exact menu at each milestone with your dealer.

## Known Issues on the Suzuki Dzire

Light, simple, and reliable, pero may konting bagay pa ring babantayan — mostly the trade-offs of a brand-new 3-cylinder:

- **3-cylinder noise and vibration (NVH).** The new Z12E triple is noticeably buzzier than the old 4-cylinder — expect a little steering-wheel shake at idle and some coarseness high in the rev range. This is the Dzire's one genuine, well-documented complaint; it's a refinement trade-off, **not a defect**.
- **CVT care.** Keep the CVT fluid fresh per schedule for long-term smoothness; avoid riding it hard in stop-and-go where you can. No widespread CVT-failure pattern reported on this car.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **Pang-ilalim** — being a light car, it's comfortable but bushings and links still wear on PH roads. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — PH heat works the system hard; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

A couple of myths worth clearing up: the new Dzire is **not** structurally unsafe — the 4th-gen scored a **5-star Global NCAP adult-occupant** rating with 6 airbags and ESC standard. And it's a **mild hybrid**, not a self-charging full hybrid — useful for efficiency, but don't expect EV-only driving. No Philippine recall was found on record for the current Dzire (it only launched here in 2025).

## Casa vs. Talyer for a Dzire

**Under warranty → Suzuki casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work.

One honest note: Suzuki's network and parts availability aren't as dense as Toyota's, so source genuine parts and confirm stock ahead — especially CVT fluid to the correct spec. Still, the parts themselves are affordable.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave CVT-fluid work and warranty service to the pros.

## Verdict: Still the Lowest Cost Per Kilometer

If running cost is the whole point, the new Dzire Hybrid is one of the cheapest sedans you can own in PH — tiny PMS bills, mild-hybrid fuel economy, and a timing chain (no belt drama). It's **sold new in 2026** (GL CVT ~₱920,000, GLX CVT ~₱998,000). The trade-off is a slightly buzzier 3-cylinder; the upside is class-leading efficiency. Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
