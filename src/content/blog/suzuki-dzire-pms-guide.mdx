The **Suzuki Dzire** is the quiet champion of low running costs — a roomy little sedan with a famously frugal 1.2L engine that sips fuel and barely needs any oil at PMS time. If your priority is the lowest possible cost per kilometer, this is one of the strongest cases in the class. Here's exactly what a Dzire costs to maintain, interval by interval. This is the Dzire companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- The Dzire runs the standard **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle.
- A regular Dzire PMS at casa is about **₱4,500–6,500** — among the cheapest sedans to service; a major 40K–80K service is **₱9,000–13,000+**. *(Average casa prices, June 2026 — verify with your Suzuki dealer.)*
- The **1.2L engine** takes only ~3.1L of oil, so a basic change-oil PMS is very light on the wallet.
- Some units use the **AGS (automated manual)** — a slight shift lurch is normal; just follow the schedule.
- **Timing chain, not belt** = walang mahal na belt replacement later.

## Suzuki Dzire PMS Cost by Interval

Costs climb at the bigger intervals when more than oil gets replaced. Ganito ang dating sa casa:

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Dzire) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–3,500 *(usually free labor)* |
| Standard PMS | 10,000–40,000 km | ₱4,500–6,500 |
| Major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱9,000–13,000+ |

The Dzire usually lands at the **bottom of the sedan range** — a tiny, efficient engine means a cheap change-oil PMS. For the full interval-by-interval breakdown of *what's actually done* at each stop, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Common Wear Items on a Dzire

Light and simple, pero may konting bagay pa ring babantayan:

- **AGS clutch behavior** (AGS units) — follow the service schedule; avoid riding it in heavy stop-and-go where you can.
- **Brake pads, wipers, battery** — normal wear; cheap and easy.
- **CVT fluid** (CVT units) — change it ~every 40,000 km for long-term smoothness.
- **Pang-ilalim** — being a light car, it's comfortable but bushings and links still wear on PH roads. Listen for `lagutok` and `kalog` — full sound guide in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).
- **Aircon** — PH heat works the system hard; expect a clean/recharge as it ages.

## Casa vs. Talyer for a Dzire

**Under warranty → Suzuki casa** (keep your receipts). **Out of warranty → a trusted talyer** is usually cheaper for the same work.

One honest note: Suzuki's network and parts availability aren't as dense as Toyota's, so source genuine parts and confirm stock ahead — especially anything AGS-related. Still, the parts themselves are affordable.

## What You Can DIY

Between visits: check tire pressure, top up washer fluid, swap the cabin filter, and eyeball your oil level. Leave AGS/CVT work and warranty service to the pros.

## Verdict: The Lowest Cost Per Kilometer

If running cost is the whole point, the Dzire is one of the cheapest sedans you can own in PH — tiny PMS bills, class-leading fuel economy, and a timing chain (no belt drama). Just don't skip the schedule and keep your records clean.
