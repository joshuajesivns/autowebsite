The **Hyundai Accent** is the value pick — well-equipped for the price, and famous for its **1.6 CRDi diesel** that sips fuel on long hauls. A quick reality check first: Hyundai **stopped selling the Accent new in the Philippines around 2022**, and while a new (gas-only) generation has been homologated, it hasn't launched yet. But thousands are still on the road and it's a popular second-hand buy — so this guide is for **current owners and used-car shoppers**. The diesel option makes its maintenance story a little different from the all-gas Japanese crowd. Here's the breakdown. This is the Accent companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- Hyundai PH runs a **5,000 km / 3-month** PMS cycle — more frequent than the 6-month interval at some rivals.
- Gas Accent: about **₱3,800–7,000** light/standard; **₱10,000–15,000+** major. The **1.6 CRDi diesel costs a bit more** per visit. *(Average casa figures, June 2026 — verify with your dealer/shop.)*
- **Diesel adds a fuel filter + water separator + diesel-grade oil**, and holds ~5.3L vs ~3.3L on the gas — budget slightly higher.
- **Both engines use a timing chain** (no belt to replace).
- **Discontinued new + smaller dealer network** than Toyota/Honda — plan where you'll service it.

## Hyundai Accent Quick Specs

| Spec | 1.4 Gas | 1.6 CRDi Diesel |
|---|---|---|
| Engine | 1.4L Gamma 4-cyl | 1.6L D4FB CRDi turbo-diesel |
| Power | ~100 PS | ~126–128 PS / 260 Nm |
| Oil capacity | ~3.3 L (5W-30) | ~5.3 L (low-SAPS synthetic) |
| Transmission | 5-speed MT or 6-speed AT | 6-speed MT or AT |
| Timing | Chain | Chain (dual) |
| PMS interval | 5,000 km / 3 months | 5,000 km / 3 months |

*Specs are for the last PH-sold Accent (the RB generation). A new, gas-only generation may return but isn't on sale yet.*

## Hyundai Accent PMS Cost by Interval

| PMS stage | Interval | Gas | CRDi Diesel |
|---|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 | ₱3,000–5,000* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱3,800–7,000 | ₱6,000–9,000* |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | ₱10,000–15,000+ | ₱12,000–18,000+* |

*Diesel figures are estimates — because the diesel was discontinued, current casa pricing is harder to pin down; confirm with a Hyundai-experienced shop.*

The diesel's extra cost comes from the **fuel filter, water-separator drain, more oil, and diesel-grade oil**. For what's done at each interval, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Accent

Most visits are **light PMS** — oil + filter and a general check, every 5,000 km. The **heavy (major) PMS** at **40,000 km and 80,000 km** adds brake fluid, coolant, transmission fluid, and (on the diesel) the **fuel filter and water-separator service** that protect the expensive injection system. That's where the bigger bills land — budget for them.

## Known Issues on the Hyundai Accent

- **Low ground clearance.** The single most-cited owner complaint — the Accent sits low, so steep humps, ramps, and flooded roads can scrape the pang-ilalim. Dahan-dahan sa hump at baha.
- **Soft, floaty suspension.** Comfort-tuned, which means a bit of float when unladen — a comfort-vs-handling trade-off, not a fault.
- **Diesel: EGR / carbon buildup.** Pure stop-go city driving lets carbon build up in the EGR and intake over time — the occasional longer highway run helps.
- **Diesel: injectors & fuel quality.** The CRDi's injectors are sensitive to dirty diesel. Buy clean fuel, change the fuel filter on time, and drain the water separator — injector repair is expensive.
- **Diesel: turbo is a big-ticket item.** Failures aren't common, but if a turbo goes, it's a pricey fix — another reason to keep oil clean and on schedule.
- **Pang-ilalim.** Bushings and links wear on PH roads; listen for `lagutok` and `kalog`. Full sound guide is in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

The honest caveat: Hyundai's network and parts supply aren't as ubiquitous as Toyota or Honda — and now that the Accent is discontinued, sort out your servicing plan early. **Stick to the casa or a Hyundai-experienced shop**, especially for the diesel's injection system. If still under warranty, casa lang muna. Comparing with the Japanese options? See the [Vios PMS guide](/blog/toyota-vios-pms-guide).

## Verdict

The Accent is a strong-value used buy, and the diesel is a genuine fuel-economy champ for high-mileage drivers — basta you're okay servicing it at a Hyundai-savvy shop and you stay on top of the diesel's fuel filter and oil. It's no longer sold new, so weigh parts access before buying. Keep records, mind the low clearance, and it's a sensible second-hand pick.
