If you're shopping for a Toyota 7-seater around the ₱1-million mark, you've almost certainly ended up staring at these two: the **Toyota Avanza** and the **Toyota Veloz**. They look related for a reason — same platform, same engine, same seven seats — kaya laging tanong: *ano nga ba talaga ang pinagkaiba, at alin ang dapat kong bilhin?* Short answer: they're mechanical twins, so the choice is really about **budget, transmission, and safety kit**, not about which one is "the better car." Here's the honest, side-by-side breakdown.

## Key Takeaways

- **Price barely overlaps.** The Avanza runs **₱844,000–₱1,071,000**; the Veloz runs **₱1,104,000–₱1,262,000**. The *cheapest* Veloz costs more than the *most expensive* Avanza. *(SRPs, July 2026 — verify with Toyota.)*
- **Same guts.** Both use the **1.5L 2NR-VE** engine, the **DNGA platform**, a timing chain, and the same 7-seat body — so space, fuel economy, and maintenance are effectively identical.
- **Transmission is the real fork:** the **Avanza offers a 5-speed manual** on base trims; the **Veloz is CVT-only**. The Avanza also has a cheaper **1.3L** engine option; the Veloz is 1.5-only.
- **Safety favors the Veloz:** **6 airbags on every Veloz**, plus **Toyota Safety Sense on the top V**. The Avanza gives you **2 airbags on three of its four trims** — 6 only on the top 1.5 G.
- **Buy the Avanza** if value and a manual matter most; **buy the Veloz** if you want SUV styling and full safety across the range.

## Which Model Years Does This Guide Cover? (2022–2026)

Both nameplates in their current form arrived together in 2022 on Toyota's **DNGA platform**: the all-new front-wheel-drive Avanza (the "W100" generation) launched in **March 2022**, and the Veloz became its own standalone model on **April 29, 2022** — before that, "Veloz" was just the Avanza's top trim, not a separate car. This guide covers the **2022 through 2026** model years of both, which have carried the same powertrains and body throughout, with only trim and price adjustments. If you're looking at a pre-2022 Avanza, that's the older **rear-wheel-drive** generation — a genuinely different car mechanically (see our [Avanza maintenance and cost breakdown](/blog/toyota-avanza-pms-guide) for that generation split).

So the first question isn't "Veloz or Avanza?" — it's *"magkano ang budget ko?"*

## Same Underneath: Platform, Engine, Space

Under the styling, these two are the same machine. Both ride on the **DNGA platform** with a **2,750 mm wheelbase** and the same **2-3-2 seven-seat layout**, so interior room and cargo space are — for all practical purposes — **identical**. Anyone telling you the Veloz is "bigger" is talking about its more aggressive bumpers and SUV cladding, not actual space inside.

The **1.5L 2NR-VE** engine (105–106 PS / 138 Nm) is shared too — same DOHC Dual VVT-i four-cylinder, same **timing chain** (walang mahal na belt na papalitan), same ~3.3L of 0W-20 oil. That means:

- **Fuel economy is essentially the same** — roughly 10–13 km/L in city traffic and 17–19 km/L on the highway for the 1.5, depending on how you drive.
- **Maintenance is the same.** Both follow Toyota's **5,000 km / 6-month** PMS cycle with the exact same parts and fluids. We break down the pesos interval-by-interval in the [Veloz PMS and cost guide](/blog/toyota-veloz-pms-guide) and the [Avanza servicing walkthrough](/blog/toyota-avanza-pms-guide) — the routine numbers land in the same small-MPV range because it's literally the same engine.

So if you were hoping one is dramatically cheaper to *run* than the other, it isn't. The differences are all in what you buy up front.

## The Real Differences

### 1. Transmission — manual lives only in the Avanza

The biggest mechanical divergence: the **Avanza offers a 5-speed manual** on its two base trims (1.3 J and 1.3 E), while **every Veloz is CVT-only**. Kung sanay kang mag-manual — or you want the lower entry price and simpler gearbox that comes with it — that's an Avanza-only option. Gusto mo ng puro auto-convenience? Both offer CVT; the Veloz just doesn't give you a choice.

### 2. Engine options — the Avanza has a cheaper 1.3L

The Avanza's base trims use a smaller **1.3L 1NR-VE** (~98 PS / 121 Nm), which is what keeps its entry price down at ₱844K and sips slightly less fuel. The **Veloz is 1.5-only** across the board. So the Avanza spans a wider range — from a budget 1.3 manual to a fully-loaded 1.5 CVT — while the Veloz starts higher and stays there.

### 3. Safety — the Veloz's clearest win

Here's where the extra money shows up. **Every Veloz — even the base E — comes with 6 airbags.** On the Avanza, only the top **1.5 G** gets 6 airbags; the three 1.3 trims make do with **2 (dual front)**. On top of that, the **Veloz 1.5 V** adds **Toyota Safety Sense** (pre-collision braking, lane-departure alert, auto high beam) plus a 360-degree camera and blind-spot monitor — a driver-assist suite **no Avanza variant offers**. If safety kit is high on your list, the Veloz makes a strong case, especially for a family hauler.

### 4. Styling & features — premium vs. practical

The Veloz is the **SUV-styled, premium-positioned** sibling: **17-inch two-tone wheels**, a larger infotainment screen, LED daytime running lights and sequential turn signals, and a more upscale cabin trim. The Avanza keeps it conventional and value-focused with **15-inch wheels** and a simpler presentation. Same body, different vibe — you're paying for how the Veloz *looks and feels*, not for more metal.

## Resale & Which One Sells

Both hold their value very well — they're Toyotas, demand is high, and either one is easy to sell later. There's **no credible PH data showing one clearly out-holds the other**, so treat resale as a wash. One practical note: the **Avanza outsells the Veloz roughly 1.8 to 1** (24,704 vs 13,745 units in 2025), so there are simply more Avanzas changing hands — which can mean a slightly easier resale and a deeper used-parts pool. The Veloz counters with its premium positioning holding appeal on the second-hand market. Call it even.

## Which Should You Buy?

Since the mechanicals are identical, decide by **budget and priorities**:

- **Buy the Avanza if:** you want the lowest price of entry (the ₱844K 1.3 J), you prefer or need a **manual**, or you just want a no-nonsense seven-seater and don't need SUV styling or a full airbag count. The **1.5 G** is the sweet spot if you want the bigger engine and 6 airbags while staying under the Veloz.
- **Buy the Veloz if:** you want **6 airbags no matter which trim** you can afford, you like the **SUV-styled premium look**, or you specifically want **Toyota Safety Sense** (go for the **V**). You're paying more, but you're getting real safety and styling upgrades, not just a badge.

The honest bottom line: there's **no wrong answer** here — it's the same reliable, cheap-to-run family machine underneath. Match the trim to your wallet and your must-haves, settle any open recalls on a used unit, and either one will serve a growing family well.
