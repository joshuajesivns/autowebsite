The **Mitsubishi Mirage** (and its [Mirage G4](/models/mitsubishi-mirage-g4) sedan twin) is built around one promise: cheap to buy, cheap to run. Its 1.2L **3A92 three-cylinder MIVEC** (78 hp, 100 Nm) sips fuel *and* oil, so the maintenance bills are among the lightest you'll find. Here's the PMS breakdown — grounded in Mitsubishi Philippines' own PMS checksheet. This is the Mirage companion to our [complete Car PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Key Takeaways

- Runs a **5,000 km** PMS cycle; on mineral oil the casa changes oil every **5,000 km / 4 months**, on full synthetic every **10,000 km / 6 months**.
- A light/standard Mirage PMS is about **₱5,000–7,000** — Mitsubishi's official **PartsCare+ standard package is ₱6,054**. *(Average casa figures, June 2026.)*
- The **heavy / major PMS** (40K & 80K km) costs more, when plugs, filters, and fluids get replaced.
- **Among the cheapest cars to maintain in PH** — small engine, ~3L oil, cheap parts.
- **Timing chain, not belt.** No expensive belt change down the line.
- **CVT fluid: officially every 60,000 km / 48 months** (many owners do ~40,000 km to be safe). Uses Mitsubishi DiaQueen CVT Fluid J4.
- **Not discontinued in PH** — still sold in 2026 and locally built in Laguna, so parts support is strong.

## Mitsubishi Mirage Quick Specs

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Engine | 1.2L (1,193cc) 3A92 3-cylinder DOHC MIVEC |
| Power | 78 hp @ 6,000 rpm / 100 Nm @ 4,000 rpm |
| Oil capacity | ~3 liters (with filter), 0W-20 |
| Transmission | 5-speed manual or CVT |
| Timing | Chain (no scheduled belt change) |
| PMS interval | Every 5,000 km (oil cadence depends on oil type) |

*Applies to both the Mirage hatchback and the Mirage G4 sedan, which share the same drivetrain and PMS schedule.*

## Mitsubishi Mirage PMS Cost by Interval

| PMS stage | Interval | Casa cost (Mirage) |
|---|---|---|
| First PMS | 1,000–5,000 km | ₱2,000–4,000 *(usually free labor)* |
| Light / standard PMS | 10,000–30,000 km | ₱5,000–7,000 *(PartsCare+ standard: ₱6,054)* |
| Heavy / major PMS | 40,000–80,000 km | higher — adds plugs, filters & fluids |

The Mirage sits at the **very bottom** of the cost range — the three-cylinder needs little oil, so a light change-oil PMS is cheap. For a standard service, Mitsubishi's published **PartsCare+ standard package is ₱6,054** (oil, oil filter, drain-plug gasket, air cleaner, cabin filter, plus labor). For an exact figure, check Mitsubishi's **PartsCare+** program or your dealer. For what's actually done at each interval, see the [main PMS guide](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

## Light PMS vs. Heavy PMS on the Mirage

Most Mirage visits are **light PMS** — oil + filter, tire rotation, and a general inspection, every 5,000 km. Cheap and quick, kasi maliit ang makina.

The **heavy (major) PMS** lands at **40,000 km and 80,000 km**. That's when the bigger-ticket items come due: spark plugs (standard plugs at 15,000 km, iridium last to ~100,000 km), air and fuel filters, brake fluid, coolant, and — critically — the **CVT fluid at 60,000 km**. More parts and labor, so budget more for these stops. The CVT service especially is cheap insurance against an expensive gearbox repair.

## Known Issues on the Mitsubishi Mirage

The Mirage is honest, basic transport — and it shows in a few recurring owner notes:

- **Cabin noise / "tinny" feel (NVH).** Road and engine noise carry into the cabin, and the thin doors and panels feel light. It's the clearest reminder of where the money was saved.
- **Three-cylinder vibration at idle.** A light shake at startup and idle, especially on CVT cars sitting in gear. Normal for the engine type, pero pansinin kung lumalala — pwedeng engine mounts na.
- **CVT behavior — respect the fluid schedule.** Some owners report hesitation or a slight judder, and a CVT repair is the costliest thing that can go wrong on this car. The fix is preventive: keep to the fluid interval (official 60,000 km; ~40,000 km if you want extra margin in heavy traffic).
- **Minor electricals.** Scattered PH owner reports of things like a weak blower or an auxiliary fan acting up after a few years — cheap to sort, but worth a listen/check during PMS.
- **Pang-ilalim.** Bushings and links wear on our roads; listen for `lagutok` or `kalog` over bumps. Full sound guide is in the [PMS pillar](/blog/car-pms-philippines-guide).

These are the trade-offs of one of the cheapest cars on the market — none are unusual for the segment, and most are inexpensive to address.

## Casa vs. Talyer & Parts

Under warranty, stick to the **Mitsubishi casa**; after, a trusted talyer is cheaper. Mirage parts are widely available at low prices — helped by the fact that the car is **assembled locally in Santa Rosa, Laguna** — kaya hindi ka mahihirapan maghanap. Compare it with the [Toyota Vios](/blog/toyota-vios-pms-guide) if you're deciding between the two budget kings.

## Verdict

If absolute lowest running cost is the goal, the Mirage is hard to beat — featherweight light-PMS bills, cheap and locally-supported parts, and a timing chain. Despite headlines about the Mirage ending elsewhere, it's **still sold new in the Philippines in 2026** and built here, so ownership is low-risk. Just respect the CVT-fluid schedule, budget for the heavy PMS at 40K/80K, and keep your records — and it'll run cheap for years.
