The landscape of South Luzon is changing rapidly. As the **SLEX TR4 extension** moves closer to completion, connecting Sto. Tomas, Batangas directly to Lucena, Quezon, we are seeing a massive shift in the automotive secondary market.

If you are a car buyer or a broker in Calabarzon, here is what you need to know about the "Expressway Effect" on vehicle valuations.

## 1. The Surge in Diesel SUV Demand
With longer, faster highway stretches, the demand for **7-seater Diesel SUVs** (like the Toyota Fortuner and Mitsubishi Montero Sport) has spiked. Commuters who previously avoided the long, winding provincial roads are now looking for "Highway Cruisers" that offer fuel efficiency over long distances.

**SEO Tip:** If you're listing a diesel unit, ensure you highlight its "Highway KM" performance.

## 2. Infrastructure-Led Appreciation
Historically, improved accessibility leads to higher local economic activity. In cities like **Lipa** and **Tiaong**, we are seeing used car prices hold their value better than in Metro Manila. Buyers are willing to pay a premium for "Provinicial-Maintained" units that haven't suffered through the stop-and-go traffic of the capital.

## 3. The "RFID Ready" Factor
A minor but growing trend in 2026 is the demand for "Ready-to-Drive" units. Second-hand cars already equipped with updated **Autosweep and Easytrip RFIDs** are moving faster off the lots. It’s a convenience factor that professional "Buy and Sell" agents are using to close deals faster.

## What's Next for Quezon Buyers?
As the TR4 reaches further into Quezon province, expect the "Manila-Standard" pricing to follow. If you've been eyeing a pickup truck for your Sariaya or Lucena business, the best time to buy is *before* the next interchange opens.
